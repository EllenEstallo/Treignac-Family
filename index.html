Artivist Retreat Participants

For four days, visionary artivists converged in Treignac, France (3–6 September 2025) for TEACHumanity’s Artivist Retreat, hosted by Pat Cruz and Sangeeta Isvaren in partnership with MAVi – Moulin des Arts Vivants Association. Together we reimagined what it means to create, to resist, and to inspire—transforming an anti-conference into a living work of art, community, and change.

Meet the participants who made this retreat unforgettable:

Gena Dawn Rabinowitz
Artist · Visual Arts · Czech Republic

Gena Dawn Rabinowitz is an international visual arts educator with more than two decades of experience in independent and international schools around the world. Her work integrates creativity, activism, and global citizenship, empowering young people to use art as a force for positive change. She is the founder of GAIA Kids (Global Artists in Action), a global initiative amplifying children’s voices and turning creativity into meaningful action. In addition to her work as an educator, Gena is a mother, photographer, expat, and world traveler, and the published author of the children’s art book The Rainbow’s Journey: An Adventure in Asia. For more information about Gena, please visit www.genadawn.com. For more information about GAIA Kids, please visit www.gaiakids.net.

Julie
Artist · Literary Arts · United States
Maria Rio
Artist · Performance · Portugal

Maria Rio is a facilitator for what she calls “Empowerment Drama for children.” She is the founder of a project called Space To Be that creates original performative moments to promote growth and empower children to express themselves through their chosen art form. By combining drama, music, movement, and mindfulness, and whatever else shows up in the creative process, she wants each child to work with what brings them joy and she supports them in overcoming their fears along the way. She feels the world can only be impacted by adults that feel good in their own skin and in order to have more of those adults, we need to empower children to believe their safe place is inside them
